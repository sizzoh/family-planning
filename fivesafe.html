CONDOMS
  • Condoms
• Male condom - Is a cylindrical rubber sheath, worn to fit over a man's erected penis before coitus to prevent pregnancy.
• Vaginal condoms - Is a cylindrical plastic sheath for fitting into vagina before sexual intercourse for the reasons as for male condoms
  • Mechanism of action
• It prevents sperm from interring into the vagina and thus preventing pregnancy.
Advantage
  • Effective, safe, reversible
    • • Prevents STls including HIV/AIDS when used correctly
    • Good option for sexual active adolescents
    • Can be used soon after childbirth.
    • Helps to prevent ectopic pregnancy
    • Can be stopped at any time
    • Can be used without see health care providers.
    • Safe, no hormonal related side effects
    • Easily to obtain at clinics and sold in many places
Disadvantages/side effects

  1. Condoms may cause itching, rash, swelling for a few people who are allergic to latex or lubricants used in some condoms
  2. May decrease sensation, making sex less enjoyable for either partner
  3. Couple may take time to put the condom on erect penis before sexual contact
  4. Small possibility that condom will slip off or break during sex o It needs proper storage to avoid weakening
  5. Female condoms are only available in some places in the country, and they are expensive if they are purchased privately
  6. Sometimes female condom makes a slight noise plastic rustling during sex
