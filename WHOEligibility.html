ASSESSMENT OF CLIENTS FOR FP METHODS

Screening Clients for FP Use 


  • To obtain clients baseline health status before service provision 

  • To detect problem which the client may have that may need management 

  • To help determine type of physical examination that must be performed

  • To determine clients suitability to family planning method.

History taking

  • Clients seeking family planning methods in clinics shall have relevant history taken as follow:
    • Social History
      1. Smoking
      2. Alcohol intake
      3. Occupation
    • Reproductive History 
      1. Menstrual cycle - last menstrual period (LMP), the flow and pattern, duration, blood loss, cycle length and dysmenorrheal.
      2. Present and past contraceptive use
      3. Reproductive needs ( current number of children, desired number and birth interval preferred.)
      4. Sexual history
      5. Relevant Obstetric history- total number of pregnancies, number of miscarriages, number of stillbirth, and children alive.
    • MEDICAL HISTORY
      1. Long term medication
      2. Disease currently under treatment

Areas to be examined and abnormal findings of physical examination

  • Head and eyes - anaemia jaundice o Neck - enlarged neck glands
  • Breasts - unusual skin colorations, lumps, retraction and dimpling of nipples, discharge any signs pregnancy
  • Abdomen - Tenderness, liver enlargement, mass, enlarged uterus, linea nigra.
  • Legs - oedema, tenderness of calf muscles
  • Vulva - vaginal discharge, ulcers, enlarged glands, odor o Vagina - colour for inflammation, ulcers, bruises and discharge
  • Cervix - colour, discharge, consistence growth and ulcers.
  • Uterus - tenderness, mobility, size and consistence
  • Urethral - discharge
  • Bartholin's glands - swelling and tenderness.

Danger Signs to Note During Physical Examination
    .
  • Yellow skin or yellow whites of the eyes an itchy skin, dark urine feeling weak suggestive of liver disease
  • Shortness of breath and wheezing suggestive of asthma
  • Shortness of breath even in slight exertion suggestive of heart disease
  • Passing of large amount of urine, feeling of thirsty or faintly suggests of diabetes mellitus
  • Swelling of the legs and ankles which forms a dimple when pressed suggestive of oedema or circulatory failure
  • Severe deep pain on one calf or thigh accompanied with swelling suggests thrombophlebitis or blood clotting
  • An itching skin or swelling of parts of the whole body after taking medicine or food indicates allergy
  • Any growth or swelling anywhere on the body suggests tumor.
  • Pain in the lower back when passing urine suggests disease of the kidney.
